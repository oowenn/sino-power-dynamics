Experience, Capability, and Delivery

Experience

Our team includes electrical engineers with experience in the U.S. power industry. They provide customized design solutions for customers and help our manufacturing partners in China understand U.S. requirements, so products meet IEEE, ANSI, and NEMA standards. We also provide U.S.-based service, and our China operations bring over 30 years of experience in the U.S. market.

Capability

Sino Power Dynamics works with three production bases in China, each manufacturing a different class of electrical product: Sanbian Technology (20kV to 550kV), Changzhou Pacific (20kV to 345kV transformers and switchgear), and Shuangxin (6kV to 145kV distribution transformers).

Delivery

Production capacity has increased significantly, improving delivery times: about 3.5 months for 550kV units, 3 months for 33kV to 345kV units, and 1 month for distribution transformers. In 2024, capacity was expanded with a new factory at Shuangxin, along with larger reserves of core materials such as silicon steel.

Project references

Two landmark projects delivered through our manufacturing network demonstrate proven capability on the most demanding utility and hyperscale data center programs in North America.

Delivered via Sanbian Technology

xAI Memphis Supercomputer Center (Project Colossus)

500kV / 161kV EHV

We delivered and energized the 500kV/161kV extra-high-voltage main substation transformers for xAI’s Memphis campus. The program demanded a fast-track manufacturing and logistics schedule. Lead time was compressed significantly while maintaining full field testing and rapid grid interconnection for the world’s largest AI cluster.

Delivered via Changzhou Pacific

Los Angeles Department of Water and Power (LADWP)

ANSI/IEEE · IEEE 693 High-Seismic

Our network designed and manufactured EHV transformers that passed LADWP’s stringent technical audits. The equipment fully complies with ANSI/IEEE standards and meets the IEEE 693 high-seismic qualification, proving mechanical and structural resilience under California’s most severe utility operating conditions.
